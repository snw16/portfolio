Background

In March 2022, Shanghai experienced a severe outbreak of COVID-19 caused by the Omicron variant, leading to large-scale lockdowns and strict public health interventions. The outbreak had a profound impact on the local economy, daily life, and attracted nationwide attention due to its spillover effects on other regions of China.

This project focuses on building predictive models to forecast the short-term trends of confirmed COVID-19 cases and asymptomatic infections in Shanghai. These forecasts assist policymakers in making timely, scientific, and effective prevention and control strategies, as well as resource allocation plans.

Objectives

  • Accurate Epidemic Forecasting: Predict short-term trends of confirmed cases and asymptomatic infections.
  • Trend Analysis: Compare predictions with historical data to uncover outbreak patterns.
  • Decision Support: Provide insights for policymakers, healthcare institutions, and the public.

Benefits

  • Improved Early Warning & Response: Enable rapid and targeted control measures.
  • Optimized Resource Allocation: Prepare for medical supply, staff, and emergency planning.
  • Enhanced Public Engagement: Transparent forecasts to build trust and cooperation.

Specific Impacts

  • Timely detection and alerts to help monitor the outbreak trajectory.
  • Efficient planning for healthcare demands and emergency response.
  • Inform targeted community interventions and optimize reopening schedules.
  • Strengthen public confidence through transparency and accuracy.

Evaluation Metrics

  • R-Squared (R¬≤)
  • Mean Absolute Error (MAE)
  • Mean Absolute Percentage Error (MAPE)
  • Root Mean Squared Error (RMSE)
  • Normalized RMSE (NRMSE)
  • Weighted MAPE (WMAPE)

Challenges

  • Data Acquisition: Required regular updates and adaptation to evolving formats.
  • Data Cleaning: Required handling of missing values, anomaly detection, and integration.
  • Crawler Reliability: Needed robustness against website structure changes.

Future Work

  • Improve data pipelines for completeness and accuracy.
  • Expand features with external data (mobility, weather, epidemiological factors).
  • Apply feature importance ranking and dimensionality reduction.
  • Develop ensemble models combining ARIMA, LSTM, and others.
  • Implement real-time forecasts for immediate risk assessments.
  • Use interpretability techniques (e.g., SHAP values) for greater stakeholder trust.
