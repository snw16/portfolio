Shanghai COVID-19 2022 Outbreak Forecast

Background

In March 2022, Shanghai experienced a severe outbreak of COVID-19 caused by the Omicron variant, leading to large-scale lockdowns and strict public health interventions. This project focuses on building predictive models to forecast short-term trends of confirmed COVID-19 cases and asymptomatic infections in Shanghai. These forecasts assist policymakers in formulating timely, scientific, and effective prevention strategies.

Objectives

  • Accurate Epidemic Forecasting: Predict short-term trends of confirmed cases and asymptomatic infections.
  • Trend Analysis: Compare predictions with historical data to uncover cyclical patterns.
  • Decision Support: Assist policymakers, healthcare institutions, and the public in strategic planning.

Benefits

  • Early Warning & Response: Enable rapid control measures to reduce outbreak impact.
  • Optimized Resource Allocation: Forecast medical supply and personnel needs.
  • Public Engagement: Provide transparent forecasts to build trust and compliance.

Specific Impacts

  • Timely detection of outbreak changes for proactive response.
  • Better preparation of healthcare systems and resource allocation.
  • Inform targeted social interventions and reopening strategies.
  • Enhance public confidence through transparent, data-driven communication.

Evaluation Metrics

  • R-Squared (R¬≤)
  • Mean Absolute Error (MAE)
  • Mean Absolute Percentage Error (MAPE)
  • Mean Squared Error (MSE)
  • Root Mean Squared Error (RMSE)
  • Normalized RMSE (NRMSE)
  • WAPE / WMAPE

Challenges

  • Data Acquisition & Updates: Required regular updates and format adjustments from JHU CSSE and Shanghai sources.
  • Data Cleaning & Integration: Managed missing values, inconsistent formats, and standardization.
  • Crawler Reliability: Maintained web scraper resilience against website changes.

Future Work

  • Data Quality: Improve pipelines for completeness and consistency.
  • Feature Expansion: Add mobility indices, weather data, and derived epidemiological features.
  • Feature Selection: Use correlation analysis to improve model efficiency.
  • Model Optimization: Experiment with ensemble models like ARIMA, LSTM, and stacking.
  • Real-Time Forecasting: Develop dynamically updating prediction systems.
  • Explainability: Use SHAP values and feature analysis for stakeholder interpretability.
